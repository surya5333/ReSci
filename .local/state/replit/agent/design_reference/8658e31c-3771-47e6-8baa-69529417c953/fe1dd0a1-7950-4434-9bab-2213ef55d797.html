Recommended Methods

Primary Method Confidence: 92%
Randomized Controlled Trial with Polysomnography

Parallel-group RCT comparing blue light exposure vs. control group using PSG for objective sleep measurement and saliva samples for melatonin analysis.

Pros

  • • Gold standard for sleep research
  • • Objective, quantifiable data
  • • High internal validity

Cons

  • • High cost ($30,000-$40,000)
  • • Requires specialized equipment
  • • Laboratory setting may affect results
Citations: Chang et al. (2015), Zeitzer et al. (2000) Feasibility: Moderate
Primary Method Confidence: 87%
Crossover Design with Actigraphy & Ecological Assessment

Within-subject crossover study using actigraphy watches and mobile apps for ecological validity with salivary melatonin sampling.

Pros

  • • Higher ecological validity
  • • Cost-effective approach
  • • Participants as own controls

Cons

  • • Potential carryover effects
  • • Less precise than PSG
  • • Compliance challenges
Citations: Reid et al. (2014), Gooley et al. (2011) Feasibility: High
Alternative Method
Backup Option
Survey-Based Observational Study with Sleep Diaries

Large-scale questionnaire study with validated sleep scales and digital device usage tracking, suitable for limited budget scenarios.

Use when: Primary methods are not feasible due to budget constraints or equipment availability
